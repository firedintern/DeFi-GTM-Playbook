# Spark: Capital as Go-To-Market

## Founding wedge

Spark launched in 2023 as SparkLend, an Aave-v3-fork money market incubated by MakerDAO and seeded directly from Maker's balance sheet via the D3M credit line. It never had a cold-start problem. **The parent supplied billions in day-one liquidity**, letting Spark undercut on rates from launch. It is Sky's first "Star."

## GTM & growth levers

- **Balance-sheet GTM.** The Spark Liquidity Layer (SLL) algorithmically deploys Sky's stablecoin reserves across chains and venues: into Morpho vaults, Aave, Ethena, and tokenized treasuries. Spark is less a lending venue than an onchain capital allocator earning spread at scale ($3.5B+ deployed; >$170M annualized revenue at peak; time-sensitive, reported: PANews research, not primary Spark disclosures).
- **Savings as the consumer hook**: sUSDS/savings vaults (fee-free; time-sensitive, reported: ~200K users, $2.45B+ AUM) distribute the Sky Savings Rate across chains.
- **Token launch as re-acceleration (June 2025)**: the SPK airdrop (300M tokens) with Ignition/Overdrive points seasons rewarding deposits, borrowing, and referrals. 65% of supply flows to users over 10 years, an unusually long-duration design. TVL peaked at $8.15B around the campaign and sits at $6.3B at snapshot.

## Distribution channels

Primarily intra-ecosystem: Sky's user base and reserves, plus the venues SLL allocates into (which are both channels and yield sources). Multichain deployment (Ethereum, Arbitrum, Base, Optimism, Unichain, Gnosis) distributes the savings product where stablecoin users already sit.

## GTM lessons (inference)

- If you have a parent with a balance sheet, liquidity seeding beats incentive farming. Rates you can actually honor are the best marketing.
- Allocator positioning converts would-be competitors into yield sources.
- Caveat: Spark's TVL swings around campaign cycles show subsidized liquidity is mercenary at the margin. The moat must migrate from rates to product before subsidies taper.

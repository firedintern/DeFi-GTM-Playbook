# Spark: Capital as Go-To-Market

## Founding wedge

Spark launched in 2023 as SparkLend, an Aave-v3-fork money market incubated by MakerDAO and seeded directly from Maker's balance sheet via the D3M credit line. It never had a cold-start problem. **The parent supplied billions in day-one liquidity**, letting Spark undercut on rates from launch. It is Sky's first "Star."

## GTM & growth levers

- **Balance-sheet GTM.** The Spark Liquidity Layer (SLL) algorithmically deploys Sky's stablecoin reserves across chains and venues: into Morpho vaults, Aave, Ethena, and tokenized treasuries. Spark is less a lending venue than an onchain capital allocator earning spread at scale (reported peak deployment near $2.8B; >$170M annualized revenue at peak; time-sensitive, reported: PANews research, not primary Spark disclosures).
- **Savings as the consumer hook**: sUSDS/savings vaults (fee-free) distribute the Sky Savings Rate across chains. User figures are largely unverifiable: ~200K users and $2.45B+ AUM circulate in secondary coverage, but onchain analysis found only ~2,800 identifiable retail wallets, with sUSDS supply concentrated among a handful of institutional holders. Treat the retail-adoption story with caution.
- **Token launch as re-acceleration (June 2025)**: the SPK airdrop (300M tokens) with Ignition/Overdrive points seasons rewarding deposits, borrowing, and referrals. 65% of supply flows to users over 10 years, an unusually long-duration design. TVL peaked around $8.2–8.6B around the campaign (sources vary) and sits at $6.3B at snapshot.

## Distribution channels

Primarily intra-ecosystem: Sky's user base and reserves, plus the venues SLL allocates into (which are both channels and yield sources). Multichain deployment (Ethereum, Arbitrum, Base, Optimism, Unichain, Gnosis) distributes the savings product where stablecoin users already sit.

## GTM lessons (inference)

- If you have a parent with a balance sheet, liquidity seeding beats incentive farming. Rates you can honor are the best marketing.
- Allocator positioning converts would-be competitors into yield sources.
- Caveat: Spark's TVL swings around campaign cycles show subsidized liquidity is mercenary at the margin. The moat must migrate from rates to product before subsidies taper.
- Caveat: a widely repeated "200K retail users" figure does not survive onchain scrutiny. Check the user-adoption story yourself rather than citing secondary coverage, especially for a protocol whose growth is otherwise parent-seeded.

# ether.fi: Narrative Surfing, Then Escaping the Commodity Trap

## Founding wedge

ether.fi rose by attaching itself to the hottest narrative of 2023–24, EigenLayer restaking, as a liquid restaking token (LRT) issuer with a differentiator: non-custodial staking where users retain key control. Its eETH let holders farm two point systems at once (EigenLayer points + ether.fi loyalty points), making it the highest-yielding "meta" asset of the cycle.

## GTM & growth levers

- **Layered points and seasons**: loyalty points scaled with ETH × time, Season 2 introduced a 10x boost, and five seasons of campaigns each ended in reward distributions. Engagement mechanics borrowed from gaming. The ETHFI airdrop (March 2024) converted points to tokens.
- **Points leverage via integrations**: Pendle markets for eETH let farmers loop points exposure; partner protocols ran "points boosting" programs, extending reach at partners' expense.
- **Product laddering**: Stake (savings) then Liquid (automated strategy vaults) then Cash (spending), explicitly mapped to a banking product suite.

## Distribution channels & the neobank pivot

Initially: the EigenLayer points economy and DeFi integrations. Then: **consumer fintech rails**. The Visa "Cash" card launched in September 2024 (spending against staked collateral: no selling, no tax event); April 2025 marked the expansion into a full neobank framing with U.S. rollout, bill pay and payroll, and membership tiers with cashback (verified: CoinDesk). On volume, be careful: a widely repeated $10M+/day figure looks overstated by roughly an order of magnitude; independent reporting clusters around $0.65–2M/day through Q1 2026.

The pivot targets the 30–50M-user CeFi/neobank market rather than a shrinking pool of restaking TVL. The aim: convert a farmed, mercenary user base into daily-active fintech customers while the position was still strong.

## GTM lessons (inference)

- Attach early to a rising narrative and out-execute on incentives, but recognize you are renting the category's growth, not owning it.
- Stacked points (yours plus the platform beneath you) outcompete single-layer rewards.
- The endgame for yield protocols is owning a daily-use relationship. Spending products convert TVL into retention.

# Aave: From Failed P2P Startup to Embedded Yield Infrastructure

## Founding wedge

Aave started as ETHLend, a 2017 ICO ($16.2M raise) doing peer-to-peer crypto loans. Matching was slow and liquidity fragmented. By 2018 it had done only ~$3M in volume. The key GTM decision was the 2018–2020 pivot: rebrand to Aave and replace P2P matching with **pooled liquidity markets**. Lenders deposit into a pool, borrowers draw instantly. Mainnet launched January 2020 and hit $1B TVL by August 2020 (verified; widely documented).

## GTM & growth levers

- **Product innovation as marketing.** Flash loans, aTokens (interest-bearing receipts), and rate switching earned developer attention and press no ad budget could buy. They made Aave the protocol builders had to understand. That is category-level developer marketing, before any explicit B2B motion.
- **Token migration + safety brand.** The LEND to AAVE migration (100:1, Oct 2020) introduced the Safety Module: staked AAVE as a backstop. Safety became the brand, structurally rather than rhetorically.
- **Liquidity mining, late and deliberate (April 2021).** stkAAVE rewards coincided with Aave overtaking Compound as the #1 lending protocol; the causal link is plausible but not directly documented (inference). Aave was a fast follower on incentives. It spent to convert leadership, not to buy discovery. The modern successor is the **Merit program**: periodic merkle-based reward distributions targeting specific behaviors and markets, a surgical replacement for blanket emissions.
- **Aggressive multichain expansion** (Polygon 2021 onward; 22 chains per DeFiLlama protocol data), capturing each new ecosystem's incentive programs as free customer acquisition.

```
Aave growth loop: innovation to embedded distribution

1. Product innovation (flash loans, aTokens, Safety Module)
        -> 2. Developer attention and integrations
        -> 3. Deep pooled liquidity, 22 chains
        -> 4. Embedded distribution (wallets, fintechs, white-labels)
        -> 5. Margin capture (GHO stablecoin, Horizon RWA)
        -> 6. Reinvest in incentives and integrations (Merit, ecosystem programs)
        -> loops back to 1

        5 also feeds -> Institutional track (2025-26): Horizon permissioned RWA,
                         Blockdaemon vaults, Kraken/Ink white-label
```

## Distribution channels

Aave's channel mix today:

- **Wallets and fintech apps**: "stablecoin earn" inside MetaMask, Bitget Wallet (80M reported users), LatAm fintechs (Lemon, Ripio, Belo, Buenbit), and commerce platforms like Whop. These users never see Aave's brand.
- **White-label deployments**: the DAO approved the Ink Foundation (Kraken-backed) white-label of Aave V3 ("Tydro") on Kraken's Ink L2, with revenue share back to the DAO (verified: The Block, Aave governance).
- **Institutional rails**: Blockdaemon made Aave Vaults the exclusive DeFi yield provider in its Earn Stack (verified: joint announcement), and J.P. Morgan's Kinexys (then Onyx) used a modified permissioned Aave in the 2022 MAS Project Guardian pilot. That one is older and narrower than sometimes reported, but it is real institutional signaling.
- **Its own front end and app**, backed by a MiCAR CASP license in Ireland for zero-fee fiat ramps.

The strategic read: Aave is competing to become the default credit substrate for third-party interfaces, not a destination app.

## Current phase (2025–2026)

2025 was Aave's best year (per Aave's 2025 Year in Review): peak deposits of $75B, ending the year at $55B. TVL stands at $23.3B on the July 2026 leaderboard view (time-sensitive; deposit and TVL methodologies differ). GHO, the native stablecoin (~$600M supply at snapshot), changes the economics of distribution: one GHO minted earns the DAO roughly what $10 of borrow does, and sGHO savings (4.25% APR) holds roughly a quarter of supply (~$148M) as sticky deposits. Owning the monetary asset lets Aave fund incentives and retention from margin instead of treasury.

Horizon (Aug 2025, with VanEck, Circle, Ripple, WisdomTree, Securitize and others) pairs permissioned RWA collateral with permissionless stablecoin supply: $570M+ deposits, the largest RWA lending market. Read Horizon as a permissioned-wrapper GTM move: the point is unlocking institutional allocation channels, not just adding asset types. V4 (approved March 2026) makes GHO the settlement asset of a hub-and-spoke architecture.

## GTM lessons (inference)

- Kill a failing model fast. The pivot, not the ICO, created the company.
- In financial products, safety is a brand. Invest in it structurally (Safety Module), not rhetorically.
- Grow margin by owning the money leg (GHO) once you own the flow.
- The largest growth channel in 2025–2026 DeFi is other people's front ends.

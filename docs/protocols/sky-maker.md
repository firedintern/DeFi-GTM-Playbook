# Sky (MakerDAO): The Power and Peril of Being the Default

## Founding wedge

MakerDAO launched DAI in 2017: the first decentralized, overcollateralized stablecoin. Its GTM was **composability itself**. DAI became the default unit of account in early DeFi, the asset in Curve's 3pool and collateral everywhere. Maker never ran liquidity mining in its formative years. Demand came from DAI's usefulness and from CDPs enabling leverage.

## GTM & growth levers

- **Monetary distribution, not marketing.** Sky competes by shaping where its stable units are held, borrowed, and reused. Yield pass-through (the Dai Savings Rate), reserve deployment, and ecosystem seeding move circulation directly. Clearest demonstration: the Enhanced DSR at 8% (Aug 2023), funded by RWA revenue, drove rapid supply growth.
- **RWA pioneer.** From 2022 Maker deployed billions of reserves into T-bills, converting a high-rate macro environment into a revenue engine that subsidizes growth. Revenue-backed incentives outlast emissions-backed ones.
- **Endgame restructuring**: the 2024 rebrand to Sky introduced USDS/SKY (1 MKR = 24,000 SKY), the Sky.money app, Sky Token Rewards, early-migration bonuses (reported variously as 1.25x rewards or a doubled Sky Savings Rate; sources conflict on the exact multiplier), and a "Stars" structure of semi-autonomous growth vehicles: Spark first, then Grove and Keel.

## Distribution channels

Historically: every DeFi venue that accepted DAI. In the Sky era: the Sky.money consumer app, sUSDS savings distributed cross-chain, and Stars as vertical-specific channels. The most distinctive recent move: in December 2025, **Keel**, a Sky-ecosystem agent deploying USDS reserves, launched the **"Tokenization Regatta"** with the Solana Foundation. It is a $500M public RFP to seed Solana-native RWAs (reported to raise Solana RWA TVL ~60%), with 40+ asset managers reportedly responding and up to $2.5B earmarked over time (verified as announced: Chainwire, Solana Compass; response figures are the organizers' claims). This is capital-as-GTM at ecosystem scale: paying issuers to build markets where USDS becomes the settlement asset.

## Current phase & honest assessment

Results are mixed (time-sensitive). The transition was slow at first: combined DAI+USDS supply was roughly flat through mid-2025 and DAI demand briefly resurged, showing how slowly preference migrates even when tokens do. Momentum then shifted: USDS grew strongly (~86% YTD by the 2026 reporting) while DAI declined as the migration completed in April 2026, the largest stablecoin conversion in crypto history. On revenue, be precise about the metric: trailing-12-month total protocol revenue is ~$240M, but the holder-specific share (actually distributed to token holders) is ~$85M, roughly 2.8x lower. Sky remains top-5 ($6.6B TVL), but Endgame's complexity is widely cited as a drag on adoption (Blockworks).

## GTM lessons (inference)

- Being the default asset is one of the strongest moats in DeFi, and a rebrand puts it at risk. Adoption lives in habit, collateral conventions and mental models, and those migrate slowly and expensively, not by contract.
- Stablecoin GTM is monetary distribution: compete by shaping circulation, not brand perception.
- Governance complexity is a GTM tax. Every layer users must understand narrows the funnel.
